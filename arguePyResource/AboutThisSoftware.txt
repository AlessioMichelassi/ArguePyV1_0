ENG:
    ArguePy is a text editor for Python programming. It is free software, distributed under the GPL license.

    It is available for Windows, Linux and Mac OS X. It was developed by Alessio Michelassi in 2023.

    The name is inspired by a famous scene of the Monty Python "Argument Clinic" written by John Cleese and Graham Chapman where Chapman enters a clinic and says "I want to argue" and Cleese replies "You're in the wrong place, this is the Argument Clinic". In Italian to argue is also synonymous with to fight and so the name was chosen to underline that programming in general is an activity that requires a lot of patience and that often turns into a discussion or a fight with the machine.
